  • Know the Area You Have Explored Around the World, Around A Continent, Or Even Around A Country!

  • Integrated with the Game Center

    Unlock Achievements in the real world and compete your exploration with other people in the Leaderboards.

  • Importing GPX Files

    You can use a separate GPS device to record your tracks and import them as GPX files through iTunes or Dropbox.

  • iCloud Support is Coming

    You will be able to see your exploration map and data on all of your iOS devices.

  1. Google Maps is used in iOS 5 and Apple Maps is used in iOS 6 and later.
  2. The app can also record your locations when running in the background. However, to save battery life of your iOS device, locations will be reported approximately every 40-50 meters when app is running in the background, and connected to the previously reported one. Therefore, no fog will be removed if you only move a few meters.
  3. Your locations will be recorded only when there are at least 3 GPS signal bars even though you entered the recording mode.
  4. Airplane Mode turns off GPS explicitly. Turn off Data Roaming or remove (or simply lock) your SIM card instead if you want to use GPS abroad.
  5. Internet connection is required to determine the corresponded countries of the recorded locations.
  6. It is recommended to use a separate GPS device to record your tracks. This would save beattery life of your iOS device and avoid GPS signal loss when Internet connection is unavailable.
  7. Auto-Lock should be turned off in Settings when importing GPX files.
  8. iCloud support is still in private beta and may take a while to be public. Stay tuned.
